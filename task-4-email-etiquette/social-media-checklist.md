# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**Prepared by:** Akhil Pratap Singh | Reg No: 25BAI11206  
**Course:** CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | VIT Bhopal University

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Keep your profile professional** | Use a clear, appropriate profile picture and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests. |
| 2 | **Think before you post** | Ask yourself if you would be comfortable with a future employer or professor seeing this content. |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings** | Regularly review who can see your posts, location, and personal information on every platform. |
| 4 | **Engage constructively** | Share useful content, congratulate peers, and participate in academic or industry discussions. |
| 5 | **Credit your sources** | Always give proper credit when sharing someone else's work, articles, images, or research. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't | Why It Is Harmful |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Don't share personal info publicly** | Avoid posting your phone number, home address, or financial details on social media. |
| 2 | **Don't engage in cyberbullying** | Refrain from making negative, hurtful, or offensive comments about anyone online — ever. |
| 3 | **Don't overshare your routine** | Avoid posting about daily schedules, travel plans, or exact real-time location. |
| 4 | **Don't spread unverified news** | Always fact-check information before sharing to avoid spreading harmful misinformation. |
| 5 | **Don't vent about teachers or institutions** | Such posts can permanently damage your academic and professional reputation. |

---

## 🔑 Golden Rule

> *"Before you post, ask: Would I say this in front of my professor, my parents,  
> and my future employer — all at the same time?"*  
> If the answer is no — **don't post it.**
