# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students in India

## 🛡️ General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
   Combine uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager if needed. Never reuse the same password across platforms.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
   This includes email, social media, and banking apps. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA blocks unauthorised access.

3. **Never click on suspicious links in emails, WhatsApp, or SMS.**
   Phishing links often look real but redirect to fake websites that steal your login credentials. Always verify the URL before entering any information.

4. **Update your apps and phone OS regularly.**
   Security patches fix known vulnerabilities. Outdated software is a common entry point for malware.

5. **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities.**
   Never log in to banking apps or enter OTPs on open networks at malls, cafes, or railway stations. Use mobile data or a VPN instead.

6. **Do not install apps from unknown sources.**
   Only download from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Many fake UPI apps and screen-sharing tools are spread via WhatsApp links.

## 💰 UPI & Financial Safety (Specific to India)

7. **Remember: Your UPI PIN is ONLY for sending money — never for receiving it.**
   If anyone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive" a payment, it is 100% a scam. Hang up or block immediately.

8. **Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or CVV with anyone — not even bank officials.**
   Legitimate bank employees never ask for OTPs or PINs over the phone or chat. No exception.

## 📞 How to Report Cybercrime in India

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (available 24×7 by phone)
- Report immediately — the faster you report financial fraud, the higher the chance of freezing the transaction.
