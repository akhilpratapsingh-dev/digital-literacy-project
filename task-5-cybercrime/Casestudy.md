# Cybercrime Case Study – UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**Type of Cybercrime:** UPI / Online Payment Fraud  
**Source:** Based on incidents reported on cybercrime.gov.in and news reports (2023–2024)

---

## What is UPI Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime in which attackers trick victims into transferring money or revealing sensitive payment credentials through deception, social engineering, or fake apps.

---

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **The Setup:** A fraudster poses as a buyer on OLX or Quikr, or pretends to be a bank official, army officer, or government representative.

2. **Building Trust:** They contact the victim via phone or WhatsApp, speak confidently, and create a sense of urgency — e.g., "I will pay you right now for the item, just accept this payment request."

3. **The Trick:** The attacker sends a **payment REQUEST** (not a payment) on UPI apps like PhonePe or GPay. They tell the victim, "You just need to enter your UPI PIN to receive the money."

4. **The Deception:** The victim enters their UPI PIN, not realising that a PIN is only required to SEND money — not receive it. The money is immediately debited from the victim's account.

5. **Disappearance:** Once the transfer goes through, the attacker blocks the victim and disappears. Recovering the money is extremely difficult.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- First-time UPI users and senior citizens unfamiliar with digital payments
- College students selling items on second-hand platforms
- Small shopkeepers and vendors
- People seeking part-time jobs or emergency loans online

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (ranging from ₹500 to several lakhs)
- Emotional distress and loss of trust in digital payments
- Difficulty in legal recovery as fraudsters use fake SIM cards and virtual accounts
- Academic distraction and mental health impact, especially for students
